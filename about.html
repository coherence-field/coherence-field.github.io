About

CFT is a new structural science that studies how systems function based on their underlying structure and the environment they’re in. It examines the interaction between internal design and external conditions, providing a structural lens for understanding coherence, drift, and system behavior across contexts.

Coherence Field Theory focuses on how coherence forms, stabilizes, and collapses across systems. It identifies the architectural forces that govern alignment, drift, and field compatibility, independent of narrative or domain.

The discipline isolates the conditions under which systems maintain structural integrity or lose it through drift, offering a framework for understanding behavior at the architectural layer.
