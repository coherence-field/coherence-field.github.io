About

Coherence Field Theory is a structural discipline focused on understanding how coherence is maintained, how incoherence emerges, and how drift propagates across systems and architectures. It examines the behavior of fields, the stability of structures, and the conditions under which systems collapse or recover.

The discipline is built on five foundational papers, each addressing a core dimension of coherence: primitives, drift, field compatibility, architectural invariants, and system dynamics under pressure. Together, they form the conceptual base layer for understanding coherence as a field-level phenomenon.
